## Operational liquidity characteristic

The pool's architecture is **JIT-replenishment**: most capital sits in admin wallet (wBTC) + BlueFin until needed. Pool's liquid USDC balance starts low and climbs each cron tick.

| Snapshot | Pool liquid USDC |
|---|---|
| Just after unpause | $0.23 |
| After cron tick 1 | $0.59 |
| After cron tick 2 | $0.88 |

**Implication for users:** small withdrawals work immediately; the largest member ($43.96 entitlement) will need to wait for the pool to replenish to at least $44 before a full withdrawal succeeds. Partial withdrawals work at any time up to the current liquid balance. The contract reverts `E_INSUFFICIENT_BALANCE` rather than silently underpaying (v0.2.0 behavior).

**Mitigation paths:**
- Per cron tick the pool gains ~$0.30 of liquid USDC from settle/replenish cycle. Full replenish for $44 withdrawal takes many ticks.
- Operator can manually accelerate by directly reverse-swapping wBTC via BluefinAggregator + the open_hedge / close_hedge transport cycle.
- Future release: cron could detect "pending withdrawal" signal and aggressively replenish.
